Examining Baltimore's 2016 rise in robberies
Adam Marton
December 2016

Baltimore has experienced a rise in robberies in 2016. Baltimore Police has assigned more detectives to work these cases as a result. Baltimore police classify robberies in one of four ways: carjacking, commercial, residential and street robberies. Examining data from Open Baltimore, it would appear that robberies are generally up across the board and in particular in two key categories: street robberies and carjackings.

See map above to explore Baltimore carjackings and street robberies in 2016

Baltimore 5,036 total robberies in 2016* as compared to 4,717 in 2015: a 6% increase across all categories. Two of those categories-- carjackings and street robberies-- were up significantly while the others -- residential and commerical -- saw declines or only slight increases.

Commercial robberies were actually down significantly year over year from 874 in 2015 down to 754 in 2016, a 16% decline in commercial robberies. While residential robberies were up slightly from last year (from 434 in 2015 to 446 in 2016, a 3% jump), these robberies are actually down 30% since 2011 (when there were 581 residential robberies). Residential robberies in 2016 were highest in the Northeastern section of the city, which has experienced a 20% rise in residential robberies since 2015.

Carjackings rose 26% from 283 in 2015 to 380 in 2016. Street robberies saw a 10% jump with 3,126 total street robberies in 2015 up to 3,456 in 2016. Since these two categories saw a precipitous jump in 2016, the rest of this analysis will focus on street robberies and carjackings.
